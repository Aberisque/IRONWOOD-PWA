General Notarization Checklist

  1. Details

    Confirm that you are not a party to the document and have no beneficial interest. Your role is to serve as an impartial witness who deters fraud by verifying identity and willingness of the signer(s). Do not offer legal advice or assess the validity of the document【254856863129904†L26-L42】.

  2. Details

    Georgia law requires the signer to appear in person before you. Remote or video notarization is prohibited; the signer must be face‑to‑face in the same room【254856863129904†L146-L158】.

  3. Details

    Use personal knowledge or a valid government‑issued photo ID. Recommended IDs include a driver’s license, passport, military ID or other government ID【254856863129904†L74-L84】. Georgia law specifically lists the Veterans Health ID card as satisfactory evidence.

  4. Details

    Ensure the document is complete and that the signer understands it. Do not verify legal sufficiency or explain its contents; only attorneys may provide legal advice【254856863129904†L136-L144】.

  5. Details

    Select the appropriate certificate (acknowledgment, jurat, copy certification, or oath/affirmation) based on the document’s requirements. Use correct wording and attach a loose certificate if needed.

  6. Details

    Sign your name exactly as it appears on your commission and record the date of the notarial act in the certificate. Apply your stamp or seal【754569454162678†L70-L77】.

  7. Details

    Charge only fees permitted by Georgia law and disclose them to the client before performing the service. Do not exploit your office for financial gain【254856863129904†L46-L49】.

  8. Details

    Effective January 1 2025, you must keep a journal for notarial acts requested by “self‑filers.” Best practice is to record all acts, including the signer’s name, address, phone number, date/time/location, ID details, type of document and the signer’s signature【471542451240252†L558-L571】.

  9. Details

    Provide the notarized document to the client. Secure your seal, stamp, and journal to prevent misuse and maintain confidentiality【254856863129904†L46-L55】.

Form I‑9 Verification Checklist

  1. Details

    Verify that the employer has appointed you as their authorized representative and provide any written instructions. This ensures you are acting on their behalf and not as a notary【395396376568428†L129-L148】.

  2. Details

    You are functioning as an authorized representative, not as a notary. Do not use your notary seal. Sign Section 2 of Form I‑9 with the title “Authorized Representative”【395396376568428†L129-L143】【180811746693912†L442-L459】.

  3. Details

    The employee must fill out Section 1 on their own, attesting to their identity and eligibility. Provide guidance if needed but do not complete it for them.

  4. Details

    Within three business days of the employee’s start date, physically examine original documents proving identity and employment authorization (e.g., passport, driver’s license plus Social Security card). Do not accept expired documents unless specifically permitted. See the USCIS instructions for accepted combinations【395396376568428†L117-L139】.

  5. Details

    Enter the document title(s), issuing authority, document number(s) and expiration date(s). Record the employee’s start date, sign and date the form, and enter your name, “Authorized Representative,” and the employer’s business name and address【180811746693912†L442-L459】.

  6. Details

    Provide the completed I‑9 to the employer. Do not retain copies unless instructed by the employer. Maintain confidentiality of the employee’s information.

  7. Details

    Since August 1 2023, employers enrolled in E‑Verify may use an alternative procedure that allows remote examination of documents【554350508279472†L330-L345】. Only follow this procedure if the employer authorizes it and provides instructions.

  8. Details

    I‑9 verification is not a notarial act. You may set your own fee, but ensure it is reasonable and aligned with market rates. Inform the employer or employee before performing the service.

Marriage Contract Guidance

Note: In Georgia, notaries cannot perform marriage ceremonies or sign the marriage license. Only authorized officiants (judges, magistrates, ministers, etc.) may solemnize the marriage and complete the license【924413961604441†L19-L28】.

  1. Details

    Ensure you are asked to witness a prenuptial or marriage contract, not the marriage license. The marriage license is completed only by the officiant【924413961604441†L19-L28】.

  2. Details

    Under Georgia law, a marriage contract must be in writing, signed by both parties and attested by at least two witnesses, one of whom must be a notary public【889013539578701†L395-L402】. Ensure the document meets these requirements.

  3. Details

    Apply the general notarization steps: require personal appearance of both parties, verify their identities with government‑issued IDs, ensure they understand the agreement, and complete the notarial certificate with your signature and seal【254856863129904†L74-L84】【254856863129904†L146-L158】.

  4. Details

    Enter the transaction in your journal (recommended) and provide the executed contract to the parties. If the contract must be filed with a court or clerk, instruct the parties accordingly.

Courier Service Workflow

  1. Details

    Confirm pick‑up and delivery addresses, contact names, deadlines and any special handling instructions before you leave.

  2. Details

    Ensure your vehicle is fueled and reliable. Prepare navigation and secure containers or envelopes to protect documents during transit.

  3. Details

    On pick‑up, verify the identity of the sender, note the time, and obtain any necessary signatures. Keep documents sealed and do not read their contents. Maintain confidentiality throughout the delivery.

  4. Details

    Travel directly to the destination. Avoid unnecessary stops. Communicate any unforeseen delays to the client promptly.

  5. Details

    Ensure you hand the documents to the correct recipient. Obtain a signature or other confirmation of delivery and record the delivery time.

  6. Details

    Immediately inform the client that the delivery is complete. Provide any required proof (signature, photo) and record the transaction in your business log for accountability.
